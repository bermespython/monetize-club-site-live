01 TOP 5 · #1 Arbitrage archetype

Dispatch / Routing Arbitrage

Capture demand fast, route fulfillment, keep the spread.

LaunchFast
CashFast
MarginHigh
LeverageHigh
RiskMed

What it is

You own the response layer, quoting layer, and coordination layer for messy urgent or short-decision-demand markets. The customer comes to you first, your system qualifies and routes, and the labor lands with fragmented providers who are slow, old-school, or operationally weak.

How it works

Buyers hit search/maps/listings first. Agents answer immediately, qualify the job, estimate purchase value, route to the best provider, and keep optimizing for conversion, payment capture, and profitable routing density. When one market underperforms, the CEO agent asks the operator to approve testing the next adjacent market instead of blindly burning spend.

Model flow loop

Traffic → response in <60s → qualification → payment or dispatch fee → provider routing → job outcome → review / referral → route-performance learning → market test approval if conversion weak

02 TOP 5 · #2 Arbitrage archetype

Lead Arbitrage

Capture high-intent buyers and sell or route the lead.

LaunchFast
CashFast
MarginHigh
LeverageHigh
RiskMed

What it is

You control paid/search/referral inbound for expensive, high-intent decisions where the real bottleneck is speed-to-lead and qualification. You monetize by lead fee, booked-appointment fee, routing fee, or a downstream conversion share where allowed.

How it works

Agents watch for high-conversion signals first: urgency, purchase readiness, qualification fit, economic value, and channel performance. They waterfall to the highest-converting approved script and handoff path available. If data is sparse, they default to the most purchase-intent-heavy segments first, then request operator approval before reallocating toward adjacent markets.

Model flow loop

Traffic → fast intake → qualification score → lead/package routing → booked consult or sold lead → downstream conversion feedback → market scoring → operator approval to pivot if weak

What it is

You find underpriced, badly marketed, distressed, or ignored opportunities before the market does. That opportunity can be a property, machine, lease, vehicle, receivable, residual book, liquidation lot, or contract position. The value is in control, packaging, and transfer.

How it works

Agents optimize around seller distress, stale attention, assignment spread, and buyer depth. They prioritize markets with real cash-buyout behavior, not pretty theory. If one market stops producing assignable spread, the CEO agent escalates a recommendation to switch to another approved opportunity class with better buyer liquidity.

Source → control / option / contract → qualify transferability → match buyer → assign or flip → collect spread → learn from stale inventory + buyer response → rotate market with approval

What it is

You sit between a confused buyer and a fragmented supply base. The buyer wants clarity and speed; the vendors want qualified demand. You standardize intake, compare offers, and capture broker fees, route fees, or markup spread.

How it works

Agents optimize for quote request conversion, quote-to-close rate, and purchase value. They use a waterfall of the best-performing supplier responses and routing logic first. If a market stops closing, they surface the next best adjacent market or vendor stack and ask the operator to approve the switch.

What it is

You acquire merchants, accounts, books, or contract relationships that throw off recurring transaction economics after the first close. The edge comes from boring recurring value, low churn, and under-optimized incumbent relationships.

How it works

Agents optimize first for highest-lifetime-value account classes, then for retention and portfolio density. They use the best historical conversion and residual-yield data available; if that dataset is thin they default to the simplest high-stability account classes first and ask for approval before chasing adjacent verticals.
