Businesses where agents outrun the incumbents.

Every model here lives in the same kind of market: fragmented, boomer-run, slow to answer the phone, worse at listings, allergic to automation. The incumbents are not sophisticated — they're old, tired, and busy running trucks.

Agents beat them to the deal. You rank higher, respond faster, brand tighter, qualify better. When a real job lands, you keep the customer relationship and the margin, then hand the physical fulfillment to a human laborer who gets paid per job. You own the demand layer. They own the wrench.

i.
Win the deal on speed and presentation

Agents answer in sixty seconds. They write better listings, run better ads, follow up relentlessly. The incumbents are missing calls, losing leads, and blaming the economy. You are not competing on craft. You are competing on coordination.

ii.
Arbitrage the work to human labor

You do not own trucks, licenses, or W-2 headcount. When a job books, you dispatch it to a local provider, a 1099 contractor, or a partnered shop at a negotiated rate. You pocket the spread between what the customer pays and what the labor costs.

iii.
Keep the customer, keep the margin

Because you own the brand, the phone number, the landing page, and the orchestration layer, the customer belongs to you forever. Reviews, repeats, referrals — all compound into you. The provider is interchangeable. The demand layer is the moat.

Urgent-Service Dispatch Arbitrage

Own the coordination layer, not the trucks.

LaunchFast
CashFast
MarginMed
LeverageHigh
SellEasy
RiskMed

What it is

You own a brand, a phone number, a landing page, and an orchestration layer. A customer with an urgent problem — locked out of their car, flat tire on the highway, broken garage door, flooded basement — finds you, reaches you in under sixty seconds, gets qualified, gets a price, and gets a provider dispatched. You collect either a service fee, a lead fee, a markup on the job, or full top-line with a payout to the provider. You do not own trucks. You own the coordination.

Ideal customer

The customer is anyone in a single-job-urgent moment. The repeat customer is a landlord, fleet manager, property manager, or small business with recurring urgent needs — that's the real expansion path.

Core offer

Simple, pre-published price bands by service type and zip code, with an "arrival ETA" guarantee. Transparent pricing beats haggling every time in urgent-service.

Unit economics

Representative numbers for a locksmith dispatch:

  • Average ticket: $140
  • Provider payout: $70–90
  • Ad spend per booked job: $25–45
  • Agent infrastructure + software: $3–5 per job
  • Contribution margin per job: $15–40

Volume is the game. A single zip in a top-50 metro can produce 400–1,000 urgent-service calls per month across all categories.

02 TOP 5 · #5 B2B revenue share

Merchant Services Statement Optimization

Save them money on every card swipe. Get paid forever.

LaunchMed
CashMed
MarginHigh
LeverageHigh
SellMed
RiskMed

What it is

You acquire merchants — typically local or mid-market businesses processing $50K to $5M per month in card volume — by offering a free analysis of their existing processing statement. You identify hidden fees, excess markups, interchange misclassifications, and non-qualified downgrades. Then you propose a better processing setup (often a different processor, or a new rate structure with an existing one), onboard the merchant, and monetize recurring payment volume.

Ideal customer

Local restaurants, salons, auto shops, retailers, eCom stores between $50K–$2M annual processing. They're big enough to have real fees, small enough to make the decision themselves, and plentiful enough that you never run out of prospects.

Core offer

> "Send us your last two processing statements. We'll send you a written analysis showing exactly what you're paying and where the excess is. If we find savings, we'll show you how to capture them. If we don't, we'll tell you your current setup is good — and walk away."

That offer converts. Because it's almost always true.

Unit economics

  • Average merchant: $250K annual volume
  • Average residual yield: 30–50 basis points
  • Average monthly residual per merchant: $60–$100
  • Lifetime value (5-year average hold): $3,600–$6,000+
  • CAC target: <$500
  • LTV:CAC target: 7:1 minimum

Book qualified calls for high-ticket sales floors. Get paid per show.

LaunchFast
CashFast

SellEasy
RiskLow

What it is

You run the top-of-funnel for high-ticket sales organizations — info-products, coaching, consulting, B2B SaaS, mastermind programs — using an agent-led engagement layer that qualifies leads, handles objections, books calls, and recovers no-shows. You bill per qualified call booked, per month, or via a hybrid rev-share on closed deals.

Ideal customer

Coaches, consultants, B2B SaaS teams, and agencies with:

  • $30K+/month revenue already (so they can pay you and feel the upside)
  • Inbound lead volume of at least 200/month
  • A real sales team or founder-closer behind the calls
  • A track record of closing on Zoom (not "it's complicated to close remotely")

Core offer

> "We book you 30+ qualified sales calls a month, guaranteed. You pay $X per qualified booking. If a booking doesn't meet our qualification standard, you don't pay."

Unit economics

  • Typical retainer: $4,000–$8,000/month
  • Cost of agent infrastructure per client: $200–$600/month
  • Cost of oversight (part-time human manager): $500–$1,500/month per 5–10 clients
  • Gross margin: 70–85%
